First Day of 2026: Cubbon Park Reflections

A quiet morning walk, memories that sit without permission, and the patient silence of trees older than regrets

Back to All Posts
1st January 2026

With:

Nilabha Jyotirmoy Apurba

Almost eleven months since I left home, and today Bangalore felt a little less unfamiliar. The city woke up slowly, like someone stretching after a long sleep. We were four—Nilabha, Jyotirmoy, Apurba, and me—walking towards Cubbon Park, carrying nothing heavy except memories that don't ask permission before sitting beside you.

Cubbon Park, Bangalore

Built in 1870 during British rule, it stands quietly in the heart of Bangalore, surrounded by government buildings, museums, libraries, and roads that never truly rest. Inside, time behaves differently.

Nearby landmarks: Vidhana Soudha, State Central Library, MG Road, and the Bengali jewellery colony

Trees older than regrets, paths worn smooth by generations of footsteps, and silence that doesn't feel empty—only patient. Nearby places like Vidhana Soudha, the State Central Library, and MG Road exist almost like witnesses, watching the park breathe while the city runs.

"The new year had just begun, and the air felt like a promise written in pencil, soft but sincere."

Families spread picnic mats, couples walked hand in hand as if hope had a physical form, and children ran without deadlines chasing them. Everyone seemed to arrive with the same invisible luggage—new resolutions, half-healed wounds, quiet prayers.

Not far from here is a Bengali colony, known mostly for its gold and jewellery craftsmanship—hands that turn metal into memory.It reminds me that roots don't disappear just because distance happens.

The first day of the year wasn't extraordinary, yet it will stay. Maybe because some moments don't announce their importance. They sit quietly, like a bookmark in a book you don't finish—but never forget.

And yes, it was fun. The kind you remember later, when life gets loud again.
